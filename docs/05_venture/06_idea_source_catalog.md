# 06 Idea Source Catalog

## Purpose

Define where new ideas should come from and how source material should be captured consistently.

The goal is not to browse randomly. The goal is to look in repeatable places where useful signals are likely to appear.

## Capture Schema

Whenever a promising signal is found, capture:

- source type
- source name or location
- affected user
- recurring pain observed
- current workaround
- data path hint
- monetization hint
- short note on why it feels promising

## Source Categories

### 1. Public Utility Pain

Look for:

- outages
- schedules
- transport friction
- permit or service delays
- information that exists but is hard to interpret

Good for:

- map-first products
- alerting products
- civic utilities
- public data backbones

### 2. Cost And Comparison Pain

Look for:

- price opacity
- supplier differences
- plan or package confusion
- hidden operational cost drivers

Good for:

- comparison products
- savings tools
- operator procurement tools

### 3. SMB Manual Workflow Pain

Look for:

- quoting
- intake and follow-up
- stock tracking
- reconciliation
- approvals
- WhatsApp-to-spreadsheet workflows

Good for:

- workflow-backed SaaS
- internal business-OS modules
- operator dashboards

### 4. Data-Rich Administrative Pain

Look for:

- repeating reconciliations
- repetitive imports or exports
- notice aggregation
- reporting and compliance tasks
- tender, billing, or schedule monitoring

Good for:

- Stitchly-first operational products
- ETL-backed products
- internal tooling with future SaaS potential

### 5. App Review And Complaint Mining

Look for:

- repeated one-star complaints
- broken expectations
- requests for features that imply real jobs-to-be-done
- evidence that people already depend on the category

Good for:

- replacement or improvement products
- focused wedges against incumbents

### 6. Community And Social Workarounds

Look for:

- community spreadsheets
- WhatsApp groups
- Telegram broadcasts
- repeated screenshots
- crowdsourced updates

Good for:

- structured alerting
- cleaned-up information products
- operator or moderation tools

### 7. Public Data And Bulletin Sources

Look for:

- government notices
- municipality bulletins
- regulator publications
- schedules and timetables
- public filings and status feeds

Good for:

- data pipelines
- monitoring products
- alerting and map overlays

### 8. First-Hand Internal Ops Pain

Look for:

- tasks already done manually in your own work
- repeatable business administration
- recurring cross-tool copying
- delayed approvals
- fragmented operations spread across chat, email, and sheets

Good for:

- business-OS modules
- Stitchly-native workflows
- founder-led validation because the user is already known

## Search Patterns

When doing live research, search for combinations of:

- location + outage / schedule / delay / complaint
- industry + manual process / spreadsheet / WhatsApp
- product category + reviews / problems / alternatives
- municipality / regulator / provider + notices / updates / feed
- South Africa + compare / tracker / alert / schedule / portal / map

The point is to search for observed pain, not only solution keywords.

## Signal Quality Heuristics

Stronger signals usually have:

- recurring behavior
- visible workaround behavior
- many affected users or high intensity for a smaller group
- a narrow but believable first wedge
- a realistic data path

Weaker signals usually have:

- only abstract market talk
- no clear user
- no obvious current workaround
- purely theoretical monetization
- heavy dependence on unreliable external data

## Red Flags

Treat signals cautiously if they depend on:

- scraping that is likely to break constantly
- data ownership that is obviously contentious
- product behavior blocked by platform rules
- very slow enterprise sales before first value can be shown
- large field operations before any lightweight validation

## Working Rule

Do not jump from one interesting article or complaint straight into build planning.

Every signal should move through:

- capture
- variant generation
- pre-score
- promotion to full scoring only if it still looks strong
